### Scope-boundary expansion — Discovered, deferred

**4. [Discovered] 135 additional title/author/gid mismatches in `corpus/books.yaml` beyond the 6 patched defects**
- **Found during:** Broader audit before applying the patch
- **Issue:** A full audit of all 240 gids in `corpus/books.yaml` (via direct Gutenberg `Title:`/`Author:` metadata probes) revealed 135 entries where the title+author labels don't match the actual Gutenberg book at that gid. Examples:
  - adventure/gid 558 labeled "The Sea-Wolf by London" → actually "The Thirty-Nine Steps by Buchan"
  - western/gid 1012 labeled "The Virginian by Wister" → actually "La Divina Commedia by Dante"
  - mystery/gid 1851 labeled "Man Who Was Thursday by Chesterton" → actually "Woman in the Alcove by Green"
  - historical/gid 7142-7170 (Walter Scott / Dumas / Sienkiewicz series) → many point to unrelated works
- **Impact:** These mismatches do NOT cause duplicate-gid issues (each wrong gid still maps 1:1 to a unique Gutenberg text). The SVM is trained on whatever text Gutenberg serves at each gid, regardless of the title label. So the model's training is internally consistent — but the genre labels may be "wrong" in the sense that, e.g., the model learns to associate the historical genre with whatever gid 7142 actually is (Thucydides' Peloponnesian War) rather than the labeled Vicomte de Bragelonne. Whether this matters for downstream macro-F1 depends on whether the actual books at those gids share genre characteristics with the intended ones.
- **Resolution applied:** Documented in this summary + `v1_to_v2_migration.md` + audit log preserved at `.planning/phases/08-corpus-expansion/wave-1-5-full-gid-audit.log`. NOT fixed — out of scope per the Wave-1.5 task's narrow scope (6 documented duplicate-gid defects only).
- **Files modified:** N/A
- **Commit:** N/A (the documentation is in the migration audit commit `749b766`)
- **Recommendation:** A future plan should EITHER (a) re-source the candidates.yaml gids by querying the Gutenberg search API for the canonical title+author combinations, then re-run `build_corpus.py` to regenerate books.yaml against corrected candidates, OR (b) accept that the broader v2 corpus is "Project Gutenberg books that broadly fit each genre" rather than "the specific named titles," and update CORPUS_SOURCING.md to reflect this. Either choice would rotate the corpus_hash again and require another full pipeline retrain.
